# Micro Gear Motors for Medical and Laboratory Equipment

## Application Overview

Medical, dental and laboratory equipment often uses compact motor-and-gearbox assemblies for pumps, valves, sample handling, positioning mechanisms, dosing systems and small automation axes. Selection must be based on the complete load, motion, environment, acoustic, service-life and verification requirements rather than size or motor power alone.

SigGear supplies micro gear motor and planetary gearbox solutions that can be evaluated as motion components for medical-device development and laboratory automation. Final motor, gearbox, shaft, feedback, cable and mounting configurations are confirmed after engineering review.

## Main Selection Factors

### Motion Function and Load

Describe the mechanism and its operating function, such as linear positioning, rotary indexing, valve actuation, pumping, sample transfer or optical adjustment. Provide the continuous load, peak load, holding requirement and complete motion sequence.

### Output Torque and Speed

State the required continuous and peak output torque, peak duration, rated speed, maximum speed, acceleration and cycle time. A higher reduction ratio may increase available output torque but normally reduces output speed.

### Installation Envelope

Provide the maximum motor and gearbox diameter, total length, shaft extension, mounting interface and cable-routing space. Encoders, brakes, connectors, housings and customer-side bearings must be included in the available envelope.

### Positioning and Feedback

Specify positioning accuracy, repeatability, allowable backlash and feedback requirements. Encoder location, gearbox backlash, structural stiffness, controller tuning and assembly tolerances all influence completed-system performance.

### Noise and Vibration

Provide the acoustic target, measurement distance, operating load, speed and installation condition. Noise and vibration depend on the selected motor, gearbox, ratio, mounting structure, control method and operating point. Published values must not be assumed without configuration-specific test data.

### Duty Cycle and Thermal Conditions

Provide operating time, rest time, start-stop frequency, load spectrum, ambient temperature and cooling method. Peak torque and peak current must not be treated as continuous ratings.

### Cleaning, Materials and Environment

State any requirements related to cleaning agents, contamination control, humidity, ingress protection, material restrictions, lubricants or sterilization exposure. Compatibility must be reviewed for the selected configuration; no cleaning, sterilization or material-compliance claim should be assumed unless it is specifically documented.

### Service Life and Reliability

Provide the expected cycle count, daily operating time, load spectrum and maintenance concept. Service-life estimates are configuration-specific and require an approved load profile and supporting test or calculation data.

## Medical-Device Integration Boundary

SigGear products described on this site are motion components. Selection of a motor or gearbox does not by itself establish compliance of the completed medical or laboratory device.

The equipment manufacturer is responsible for the completed-system requirements, including applicable risk management, electrical and mechanical safety, EMC, software and control validation, cleaning or sterilization validation, material and biocompatibility review, traceability, labeling and regulatory approval.

Component documentation is supplied according to the selected model and agreed project scope. Do not infer medical-device certification, sterilization compatibility or biocompatibility from general company or quality-management certificates.
