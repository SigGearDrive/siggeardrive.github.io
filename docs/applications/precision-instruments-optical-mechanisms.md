# Gear Motors for Precision Instruments and Optical Mechanisms

## Application Overview

Precision instruments, optical systems and inspection equipment often use compact geared motion for focus adjustment, zoom mechanisms, filter wheels, rotary stages, pan-and-tilt axes, aperture control, sensor positioning and small indexing mechanisms.

SigGear supplies micro gear motor and miniature planetary gearbox solutions that can be evaluated for these applications. Final motor, reduction ratio, shaft, bearing, feedback and mounting configurations are confirmed after engineering review.

## Main Selection Factors

### Motion Function and Travel Range

Describe the mechanism and required motion, including rotary or linear travel, operating angle, number of turns, indexing positions, end stops and homing method. The transmission should be selected from the complete motion sequence rather than a single speed or torque value.

### Output Torque and Speed

Provide continuous torque, peak torque, peak duration, rated speed, maximum speed, acceleration, deceleration and cycle time at the gearbox output. Friction, preload, seals, cable forces and external mechanisms must be included in the load estimate.

### Positioning Accuracy and Repeatability

State the required positioning accuracy, repeatability, angular resolution and settling behavior of the completed mechanism. Gearbox backlash is only one contributor; encoder location, structural stiffness, bearing clearance, controller tuning and assembly tolerances must also be evaluated.

### Backlash and Direction Reversal

Specify whether the mechanism operates mainly in one direction or reverses frequently. Reversal performance depends on gearbox backlash, preload, compliance, friction and control strategy. Published performance must be confirmed for the selected gearbox size, ratio and stage configuration.

### Radial, Axial and Overturning Loads

Provide pulley, gear, belt, lead-screw, lens-group or platform loads acting on the output shaft. Radial load, axial load and overturning moment must be reviewed separately from output torque. External bearings may be required when the customer mechanism places significant cantilevered load on the gearbox output.

### Noise, Vibration and Image Stability

For imaging, sensing or laboratory systems, provide the acoustic target and any vibration or image-stability requirement. Noise and vibration depend on the motor, gearbox, ratio, speed, load, mounting structure and controller. Configuration-specific test data is required before a numeric claim is made.

### Encoder, Limit and Homing Requirements

Confirm whether the system requires motor commutation feedback, output-position feedback, incremental or absolute position sensing, limit switches, index marks or a homing routine. Feedback options depend on the selected motor and mechanical configuration.

### Duty Cycle and Environment

Provide operating time, rest time, start-stop frequency, ambient temperature, contamination exposure, humidity, vacuum or clean-environment requirements and the proposed cooling method. No vacuum, cleanroom, material or lubricant compatibility should be assumed unless it is specifically documented for the selected configuration.

## Precision-Performance Boundary

A motor or gearbox component does not by itself establish the positioning accuracy, repeatability, resolution or image stability of the completed instrument. The equipment designer must evaluate the complete mechanical stack, encoder arrangement, bearings, structure, control system, calibration method and operating environment.

Specifications from one gearbox diameter, ratio or stage configuration must not be transferred to another without confirmation. Detailed backlash, noise, life, efficiency and load information is supplied according to the selected configuration and available supporting data.
