# SigGear Quotation Readiness Checklist

Use this checklist before issuing a formal quotation or making a technical commitment.

## Customer and Project

- Company and country identified
- Application and exact installation position understood
- Project stage confirmed
- Technical decision maker or responsible contact identified
- Prototype schedule and supplier-selection date understood
- Preferred contact method recorded privately

## Technical Requirement

- Continuous torque confirmed
- Peak torque and peak duration confirmed
- Rated and maximum speed confirmed
- Voltage and current availability confirmed
- Size and weight limits confirmed
- Radial, axial and overturning loads reviewed
- Duty cycle, temperature and cooling understood
- Backlash, accuracy, noise and life targets stated
- Driver, encoder, communication, protocol and control modes defined
- Brake, holding and power-off behavior defined when applicable

## Product and Configuration

- Canonical model page reviewed
- Product master-data record reviewed
- Sales usage and prohibited shortcuts checked
- Exact model, ratio and hardware configuration identified
- Included and excluded components listed
- Optional electronics, cables and connectors identified
- Customer-specific modifications listed
- Controlled drawing or internal technical source reconciled privately
- Engineering reviewer and approval status recorded privately

## Commercial Inputs

- Prototype quantity confirmed
- Estimated annual quantity confirmed
- Customization scope confirmed
- Required testing and documents confirmed
- Delivery destination confirmed
- Freight basis and shipping terms confirmed privately
- Price, tooling, payment terms and lead time approved through the private quotation process

## Documentation

- Correct datasheet or public product link selected
- Drawing or CAD file matches the quoted configuration
- Test evidence matches the model and conditions
- Certification claim matches the certificate scope
- Quotation states configuration assumptions and exclusions

## Readiness Outcome

- **Ready for formal quotation:** technical configuration and commercial inputs are complete.
- **Preliminary quotation only:** assumptions and unresolved items are stated clearly.
- **Engineering review required:** technical feasibility or customization is unresolved.
- **Not ready:** critical application, quantity or delivery information is missing.
