# SigGear Sales and Inquiry Knowledge

This section provides a controlled public workflow for qualifying customer requirements, selecting a preliminary product candidate and preparing a safe response. It is intended for sales staff, distributors and AI-assisted inquiry handling.

## Workflow

| Stage | Purpose | Guide |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Qualify | Collect the minimum information needed to understand the project | [Customer qualification checklist](customer-qualification-checklist.md) |
| 2. Match | Compare the requirement with canonical SigGear product pages | [Product matching workflow](product-matching-workflow.md) |
| 3. Respond | Answer the customer first, state boundaries and request missing information | [Inquiry response policy](inquiry-response-policy.md) |
| 4. Draft | Use a controlled structure without inventing parameters or commercial terms | [Reply templates](reply-templates.md) |
| 5. Quote | Confirm technical and commercial readiness before issuing a formal quotation | [Quotation readiness checklist](quotation-readiness-checklist.md) |

## Core Rule

A public product page supports preliminary selection. It does not by itself authorize a final technical commitment, price, delivery time, customization promise or production release.

Use the [product model index](../knowledge-base/product-model-index.md), machine-readable product records and [sales usage review matrix](../knowledge-base/sales-usage-and-review-status.md) together.

## Public and Private Boundary

This public repository may contain generic inquiry questions, response structures and approved public product information.

Do not add:

- Customer names, addresses, contact details, messages or drawings
- Customer-specific requirements that could identify a project
- Prices, discounts, internal MOQ rules, costs or margins
- Private quotations, payment terms or negotiation strategy
- Unreleased product data, restricted drawings or NDA material

Customer-specific work belongs in an approved private project or customer-management system.
