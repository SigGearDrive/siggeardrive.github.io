## Why Servo Motors Need Planetary Gearboxes

Servo motors, stepper motors, brushless motors, and DC motors often operate at higher speed than the final mechanism requires. A planetary gearbox helps reduce speed and increase output torque while keeping the drive system compact.

A suitable planetary gearbox can help improve:

* Output torque
* Low-speed control
* Motion stability
* Positioning performance
* Compact motor integration
* Load carrying capability
* Mechanical layout flexibility
* Prototype development speed
* Long-term drive reliability

For compact automation systems, robotic mechanisms, medical devices, lab automation, optical equipment, and smart hardware, the gearbox should be selected according to motor type, torque, speed, gear ratio, backlash, duty cycle, and installation space.

## Selection Parameters

When selecting a planetary gearbox for servo motors or other motor-driven systems, engineers should evaluate both motor and load requirements.

| Parameter              | Notes                                                                                      |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |

## Servo Motor Gearbox Design Considerations

Servo motor gearbox selection is not only about torque. Engineers should also consider positioning accuracy, inertia matching, control response, and mechanical connection.

| Design Factor    | Typical Consideration                                                            |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Gear ratio       | Higher ratio increases output torque but reduces output speed.                   |
| Backlash         | Important for accurate positioning and repeatability.                            |
| Motor inertia    | Gearbox selection can affect system response and control stability.              |
| Output load      | Confirm radial load, axial load, and moment load where applicable.               |
| Shaft connection | Poor coupling design can affect vibration, accuracy, and lifetime.               |
| Duty cycle       | Frequent start-stop or high acceleration may increase heating and wear.          |
| Noise            | Low-noise gearbox design is important for indoor and precision equipment.        |
| Sample testing   | Prototype testing helps confirm torque, speed, noise, temperature, and lifetime. |
